Michigan Language Center: Website for ESL Users

Michigan Language Center (MLC) has been looking to increase their application numbers to international populations and needed our help to make their website more accessible to those who do not speak English as a first language. Our team of UX Design students had the responsibility of creating a landing page specifically for Korean students based on background research, user interviews, A/B Testing, and design.

Migrant Education Initiative

Proud co-creator of the Migrant Education Initiative (MEI). MEI attracts more migrant students to the University of Michigan with direct recruitment efforts. We are partnered with Southwest Michigan school districts and have two recruitment techniques; a migrant campus day and small scholarship fund.

Design Recommendations for Ann Arbor Parks & Rec

During the Spring 2018 semester, I was a student in a UX Field Research class. My three group-mates and I had to come up with a solution for park goers to easily receive park & event information in three weeks. In that time span, we conducted interviews with Ann Arbor park goers, created a competitive analysis, and completed an affinity map to find an innovative solution that would benefit Ann Arbor park goers and the city council.

Comerica Millennial Innovation Competition

From May-August 2018, I interned for Comerica Bank in the UX department. During the internship, all interns and recent grads got the chance to participate in Comerica Millennial Innovation Jumpstart Week, a competition aimed to draw in more millennials to the bank. The competition lasted a week and took place in Ann Arbor, MI. We were placed into teams based on our innovative thinking, and were to create a product that aligns with millennial expectations when banking.

#MADHacks@Oracle

The #MADHacks@Oracle competition is a hack-a-thon hosted by Oracle for Oracle employees to create innovative solutions and applications while also integrating Oracle products. My team and I came up with an idea to better internal communications between Oracle employees through a system that would allow users to display their skills while also advertising skills they are seeking to learn more about.

Weather Predicting Chatbot

In this project, I used the Google Maps and DarkSky APIs along with caching to create a weather predicting chat bot that would work on and offline. Functions included computing probability of rain, coldest temperatures, and hottest temperatures over the course of the day along with the week. The commands for this chat bot were pre-determined by me in the functions I wrote in my code. The code also detected if a fake city was input. The programming system used was Python 2.
